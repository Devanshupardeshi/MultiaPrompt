### 1. Compititor Research Brand Stratergy

### Prompt:

Act as a senior brand strategist and competitor research analyst.

I want you to research and analyze competitors for the following brand/business:

Business Name: [Enter business name]
Industry: [Enter industry]
Location/Market: [Enter target location/market]
Target Audience: [Enter target audience]
Client Preferences/References: [Enter any competitor websites, brand styles, or design references liked by client]

Your task is to create a competitor list and analyze each competitor based on the following pointers:

1. Competitor Positioning
- How does the competitor present their company?
- What is their industry positioning? For example: premium, affordable, niche, tech-driven, service-focused, luxury, mass-market, etc.
- What tone of communication do they use? For example: formal, casual, innovative, emotional, corporate, friendly, bold, etc.
1. Messaging Strategy
- What is their core message?
- Mention their tagline, slogan, or value proposition if available.
- Analyze whether their communication is clear, strong, memorable, or weak.
1. Branding Consistency
- Check consistency across website, social media, pitch material, and other visible platforms.
- Observe logo, colors, typography, imagery, and communication tone.
- Mention whether the brand is consistent or inconsistent.
- Insight angle:
    - If inconsistent, identify opportunity to stand out with stronger branding.
    - If consistent, suggest how our brand can differentiate creatively.
1. Service Presentation
- How are their services or products explained?
- Is the information detailed, minimal, technical, or benefit-focused?
- Do they use visuals, icons, storytelling, case studies, or process diagrams?
1. Content Structure
- How is their information organized?
- Analyze the content flow: introduction, problem, solution, services, proof, CTA.
- Comment on website or pitch layout hierarchy.
1. Content Styling & Design
- Analyze color palette usage.
- Analyze typography style, font hierarchy, and readability.
- Mention highlighting techniques such as bold text, icons, cards, sections, images, or animations.
- Comment on layout style: clean, cluttered, modern, minimal, premium, traditional, etc.
1. Overall Branding Style
- Define their brand personality: corporate, youthful, luxury, techy, modern, traditional, emotional, etc.
- Check visual identity coherence.
- Identify whether the brand focuses more on emotional appeal or functional benefits.
1. Client Preference Integration
- Identify design styles, content patterns, or branding elements that the client may like from these competitors.
- Suggest how we can customize branding based on client taste and industry standards.
- Mention what to adopt, what to avoid, and what to improve.

Output Format:

Create the response in table format with these columns:

Competitor Name | Website/Platform | Positioning | Messaging Strategy | Branding Consistency | Service Presentation | Content Structure | Content Styling & Design | Overall Branding Style | Client Preference Insight | Opportunity for Our Brand

After the table, provide:

1. Top 5 competitor insights
2. Common branding patterns in the industry
3. Gaps or weaknesses in competitor branding
4. Differentiation opportunities for our brand
5. Recommended branding direction
6. Suggested tone of voice
7. Suggested visual style
8. Final strategic recommendation

Keep the analysis practical, accurate, and useful for a branding, website design, and strategy team.
Use clear language and avoid unnecessary theory.

### 1. Compititor Research Design Stratergy

### Prompt :

Act as a Senior UI Designer and Brand Research Expert.

I will provide the following inputs:

1. Brand Name
2. Industry / Business Category
3. Target Audience
4. Location / Market Region

Based on these inputs, conduct competitor research from a UI/visual design perspective and identify the top 5 competitors in the same industry and market.

For each competitor, analyze the following points:

1. Competitor Overview
- Brand name
- Industry positioning
- Target audience
- Market presence
1. Visual Identity Analysis
- Color palette
- Typography style
- Logo style
- Marks and symbols
- Icon style
- Patterns, textures, or visual elements
- Illustration or image style
1. Website / UI Design Analysis
- Layout style
- Navigation structure
- Section hierarchy
- Use of whitespace
- CTA style
- Mobile responsiveness
- Overall user experience
1. Branding Strengths
- What they are doing well visually
- Their design consistency
- Strong brand recall elements
1. Design Gaps / Weaknesses
- What is missing in their visual identity
- UI/UX issues
- Lack of differentiation
- Weak content or design flow
- Inconsistent branding elements
1. Opportunity for Our Brand
- How our brand can visually stand out
- Color, typography, logo, and UI direction we can use
- Design advantages we can build
- Unique visual positioning recommendation

Output Format:

Create the response in a structured table format with these columns:

| Competitor | Positioning | Color Palette | Typography | Logo / Marks / Symbols | Patterns / Textures | UI Design Style | Strengths | Gaps / Weaknesses | Opportunity for Our Brand |

After the table, provide:

1. Common design trends in this industry
2. Visual gaps found across competitors
3. Recommended UI design direction for our brand
4. Suggested color palette direction
5. Suggested typography direction
6. Suggested logo and symbol direction
7. Final design advantage strategy

Structure the widget in exactly these 9 sections:

00 — HEADER: title, brand name, 4 metadata pills (industry, type, location, audience), context note on a left-border-accent block explaining how the landscape splits.

01 — LANDSCAPE: 5 small competitor cards in a grid, each with number, name, one-line positioning, and 4 real-brand color swatches.

02 — POSITIONING MAP: SVG scatter plot (viewBox 0 0 600 420) with two labeled axes appropriate to the category. Plot 5 competitors as colored circles (c-coral, c-pink, c-amber, c-blue, c-gray). Add a dashed c-teal rectangle in the genuine whitespace showing where our brand sits.

03 — DEEP-DIVE: 5 large cards, one per competitor. Each contains: numbered badge + name + tagline; OVERVIEW paragraph (3–5 sentences on founding, positioning, audience, market); VISUAL IDENTITY paragraph (palette, type, logo, patterns, illustration); WEBSITE/UI paragraph (layout, nav, hierarchy, whitespace, CTAs, mobile, UX); two-column STRENGTHS/GAPS block with check/x icons; OPPORTUNITY callout box tinted to the competitor's color (2–3 sentences).

04 — TRENDS & GAPS: two side-by-side cards on secondary background — "Common design trends" and "Visual gaps across competitors" (4–6 sentence paragraphs each).

05 — RECOMMENDED UI DIRECTION: 1-sentence positioning statement + single white card with 3 paragraphs (layout philosophy, key UI moves for this audience, overall UX feel).

06 — COLOR PALETTE: white card with 5 swatches in a grid (72px color block + name + hex in mono + 1-line role). Rationale paragraph below.

07 — TYPOGRAPHY: two side-by-side cards — DISPLAY (large serif sample of brand name + description + fonts in mono) and BODY/UI (realistic body-copy sample + description + fonts in mono). Optional small note card on accent type.

08 — LOGO & SYMBOL: white card with a custom SVG logo concept for the brand name + rationale paragraph (mark explanation, category clichés to avoid, reference category).

09 — STRATEGY: dark block (rich dark from the recommended palette) with cream text. Eyebrow label, large one-line headline naming the strategic insight, 3–4 sentence summary, and 4 pillar cards in a grid (PILLAR XX label + name + 1-sentence rationale).

DESIGN RULES:

- CSS vars for neutrals; var(--border-radius-lg) for cards, -md for pills
- Borders: 0.5px solid var(--color-border-tertiary)
- Tabler outline icons only
- Sentence case throughout; ALL CAPS only for eyebrow labels
- Font weights 400 and 500 only — never 600/700
- Eyebrow: 11px, letter-spacing 0.1em, secondary text color
- Section headings: 20px / 500
- Body: 13px / line-height 1.6–1.7
- Tinted callouts: 50-stop background + 800/900-stop text from same ramp
- No gradients, shadows, or decorative effects
- All competitor data from real web research

## Design Prompting

### 1. Web Site StyleGuide

### Prompt :

Act as a Senior Brand Strategist and Senior UI/UX Designer.

I will provide competitor research references, brand strategy research, and design research. Based on that input, create a complete website strategy and visual design direction for my brand.

Your task is to analyze the competitor references from a branding and design perspective and suggest a strong, differentiated website strategy.

Create the output in a clear, structured format covering the following aspects:

1. Color Palette
Suggest primary, secondary, and tertiary colors based on the brand personality, target audience, industry, and competitor gaps. Include color combinations and explain where each color should be used.
2. Typography
Suggest font styles for headings, subheadings, body text, buttons, and captions. Recommend good font pairings, font sizes, font weights, and usage of bold, italic, or highlight styles for hierarchy and emphasis.
3. Hero Banner
Suggest hero banner layout options with text placement, image/video placement, CTA placement, background style, and visual hierarchy. Include at least 3 hero banner concepts.
4. Website Sections
Suggest dedicated sections for:
- Services
- About Us
- Case Studies
- Blog
- Contact

For each section, recommend layout style, content flow, visual treatment, and user experience purpose.

1. Imagery Style
Suggest the type of images to use such as professional photography, illustrations, graphics, AI-generated visuals, abstract visuals, or minimal visuals. Explain the mood and brand impression each style creates.
2. Iconography
Suggest icon style or custom icon direction for services, features, process, benefits, and navigation. Mention whether icons should be line-based, filled, 3D, minimal, geometric, or illustrative.
3. Logo Placement
Suggest how the logo should be placed and used across the website, including header, footer, hero section, favicon, watermark, and background branding elements.
4. Background Elements
Suggest background patterns, textures, gradients, overlays, shapes, or visual elements that can add depth and premium feel without making the design cluttered.
5. Animations and Interactions
Suggest subtle animations, hover effects, scroll animations, transitions, micro-interactions, button effects, and card interactions to improve user experience.
6. Testimonials / Client Showcase
Suggest layout ideas for testimonials, client logos, success stories, case studies, ratings, or client feedback to build trust and credibility.
7. eContent Presentation
Suggest different content presentation formats such as grids, cards, lists, timelines, sliders, tabs, accordions, or comparison blocks for services, products, portfolio, and case studies.
8. Footer Design
Suggest footer layout including navigation links, contact details, social media links, newsletter section, CTA, legal links, and brand statement.
9. Callout Boxes
Suggest eye-catching callout box styles for key messages, offers, achievements, statistics, benefits, or important announcements.
10. Social Proof
Suggest how to include social media links, client logos, numbers, testimonials, follower count, media mentions, certifications, awards, or partnership badges.
11. Video Integration
Suggest where videos can be used on the website, such as hero banner, about section, product demo, case study, testimonial, or background video. Mention how video should be visually integrated.
12. Forms
Suggest contact form and inquiry form design style, required fields, layout, button style, validation style, and user-friendly form flow.
13. Navigation Style
Suggest suitable navigation style such as sticky header, mega menu, simple top navigation, side menu, hidden menu, or mobile-first hamburger menu based on website complexity.
14. Content Cards
Suggest how to organize services, features, blogs, team members, case studies, or portfolio items into cards or panels with strong visual hierarchy.
15. Featured Content
Suggest sections for featured blogs, latest news, updates, case studies, achievements, or important resources to keep visitors informed and engaged.
16. Buttons and CTAs
Suggest CTA button styles, sizes, shapes, colors, hover effects, placement, and CTA text ideas. Include primary, secondary, and tertiary CTA suggestions.

Also include:

A. Competitor Design Gap Analysis
Identify what competitors are doing well and what they are lacking in terms of website design, branding, UI/UX, content structure, visual identity, and conversion strategy.

B. Brand Differentiation Strategy
Suggest how our website can look more unique, premium, trustworthy, modern, and conversion-focused compared to competitors.

C. Recommended Website Design Direction
Give one final recommended design direction including:

- Visual theme
- Color mood
- Typography mood
- Layout style
- Image direction
- Animation style
- Overall brand personality

D. Final Website Structure
Provide a suggested homepage structure from top to bottom:
Hero Section → Trust Section → Services → About → Case Studies → Testimonials → Blog → CTA → Contact → Footer

Output Format:
Use clear headings, bullet points, and tables wherever required.
Keep the language professional, practical, and useful for UI designers, brand strategists, and content teams.
