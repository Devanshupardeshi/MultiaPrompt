Why most AI-generated
designs look cheap.
You've seen them. The landing pages that look like they were made in 2019. Generic
fonts, flat colors, lifeless layouts. The kind of page that screams "AI did this" — and
not in a good way.
Here's the truth: the AI isn't the problem. The prompt is.
Most people treat AI design tools like a vending machine — put in a vague request,
hope something decent comes out. "Make me a dark landing page for a tech startup."
And you get exactly what you asked for. Technically correct. Completely forgettable.
The designers getting jaw-dropping results aren't more creative than you. They're
more specific than you.
They've learned that AI works like a junior designer — incredibly fast, technically
capable, but completely dependent on the brief you give it.
This ebook teaches you that brief. A 5-layer framework that transforms your prompts
from vague instructions into a creative director's brief — the kind that produces
pages that feel cinematic, premium, and intentional.
Think of it like directing a junior designer who's incredibly fast but
needs clear direction. Give it clarity, and it gives you craft.
By the time you finish this guide, you'll know exactly how to specify fonts, colors,
glass effects, layouts, and motion in a way that makes AI produce work you're
genuinely proud of. Let's begin.

FONTS
Fonts: name them.
Both of them.
The single fastest way to elevate any AI design.
Every AI tool has a default. And that default is almost always Inter or Roboto — clean,
neutral, completely unremarkable. When you say "use a nice font," you're handing
the wheel to the algorithm, and the algorithm plays it safe.
The fix is embarrassingly simple: name the fonts yourself. Not "a serif headline font."
Not "something elegant." The actual name. The actual weights. The actual Google
Fonts URL.
WHY TWO FONTS?
One font does one thing. A combination of two — a distinctive heading face and a
refined body face — creates hierarchy, personality, and contrast. It's the difference
between a page that feels designed and a page that feels generated.
THE FORMULA THAT WORKS
Pair an expressive, characterful headline font with a clean, legible body font. Italic
display + light grotesque creates a luxury editorial feel.

COLOR
Stop saying "dark
background."
Exact values, not vibes. A system, not a color.
Every beginner prompt says "dark background." The AI hears this and gives you
#1a1a1a or #222222. That's dark grey. And dark grey is not cinematic. True black —
#000000 or very close — is what separates cheap from premium.
But color isn't just the background. It's the entire opacity system that creates depth.
THE OPACITY HIERARCHY
On a dark, premium page you rarely use flat white text. You build a layered system at
different opacities. The eye knows exactly where to look.

HOW TO SPECIFY COLOR
Use CSS custom properties in HSL. Define every value, including the opacity
hierarchy. Give the AI a system, not a color — it produces consistent results across
every component.

GLASS EFFECTS
Bad glass, and
liquid glass.
Described physics, not buzzwords. Real glass catches light at the edges.
Glassmorphism is everywhere in 2026 — which means most of it looks the same. A
blurry box with a white border. That's not liquid glass. That's a CSS tutorial.
Liquid glass catches light. It has gradient borders that fade in the middle, simulating
real refraction. It has different strengths for different contexts. It behaves like
a material.
TWO VERSIONS YOU ALWAYS NEED
SUBTLE · CARDS, NAVBAR, BADGES
backdrop-filter: blur(4px). Gradient border (no solid edge). Catches light at the edges.
STRONG · CTA BUTTONS, PROMINENT UI
backdrop-filter: blur(50px). Brighter gradient border. Box-shadow for depth. Weighty,
premium.

LAYOUT · SKELETON
Layout: give it a skeleton.
Don't say "clean layout." Name every section. Describe its structure.
This is where most intermediate prompt writers fail. They nail the fonts, colors, and
glass — and then write "clean modern layout with hero, features, footer." The AI does
its best, and its best is generic. Layout is architecture. You wouldn't hand an architect
a brief that says "nice building." You'd hand them a floor plan. Section by section.
Element by element.
01
SECTION · NAVBAR
Floating glass pill
Fixed, floating above content. Logo left. Nav links inside a glass pill,
centered. Solid white CTA as the last item in the pill, with an ArrowUpRight
icon. The pill shape alone signals "premium."
02
SECTION · HERO
Cinematic first impression
1000px tall. Video or image background. Badge pill above headline ·
animated headline · subtext with delay · two CTAs (one glass, one text-
only) · partners bar at bottom: "Trusted by Stripe, Vercel, Linear."
03
SECTION · FEATURES
The chess layout
Alternate sides instead of card grids. Text left → media right · then flip ·
text right → media left. Each row carries a title, body paragraph, "Learn
more" glass button, and media in a glass container. Visual rhythm replaces
monotony.

SECTION · STATS
Desaturated video moment

05
SECTION · TESTIMONIALS
Trust layer
Three-column grid. Glass cards. Quote in italic, lighter weight. Name and

06
SECTION · CTA + FOOTER
The close
Full-width video background. Large italic headline. Two buttons: one glass,
one solid white. The contrast is intentional — glass for primary, solid for
secondary. Solid white reads louder, anchoring the eye against the moving
video behind it.
The more sections you blueprint, the less the AI has to guess. Every
guess it makes is a risk of getting it wrong.

CH 05 · MOTION

MOTION · TIMING
Motion: timing like a
creative director.
"Add animations" gets you bouncing buttons. This gets you a page that breathes.
Most beginners either skip motion or write "add smooth animations." Both produce
something that moves but doesn't feel intentional. The difference between a page
that loads and a page that arrives is choreography — sequence, timing, and restraint.
THE MOTION LANGUAGE
01
Headlines · word-by-word blur
Each word from blur(10px)·y:50·opacity:0. 100ms stagger.
IntersectionObserver-triggered.
02
Subtext · delayed blur fade
Same fade as a unit. Delay 0.8s. Duration 0.6s.
03
CTA buttons · last to enter
Same blur-fade. Delay 1.1s. Headline → subtext → action.
04
Video · faded edges
200px gradient overlays top + bottom. Hero bottom uses 300px.
05
Stats video · desaturate
filter: saturate(0) — strips color so numbers pop.
Motion isn't decoration. It's sequence.

THE MINDSET SHIFT
Stop thinking of prompts as instructions. Start thinking of them as briefs. A brief is
what a creative director hands to a designer before a project begins. It says: here are
the fonts, here's the color system, here's how the glass effects work, here's the
section-by-section structure, here's how everything moves.
The AI is your junior designer. Exceptionally fast. Technically skilled. Completely
dependent on the brief.
